Tobacco Smoking

The rise of Tobbaco smoking

Smoking is one of the most highly addictive forms of recreational drug use which is being practised by over one billion people globally. Tobacco was largely practised by people especially young adults when it was first invented but over the years, the perception surround smoking as changed. This could be attributed to the fact that the adverse effect of this act became more prominent. Consequently, this act came to be viewed in a decidedly negative light, especially in western countries. Its rise has become much more observable in recent years.

Impact of Tobbaco

Tobacco smoking is a major cause of one of the world's largest health problems. Throughout the 20th century, this tobacco smoking killed around 100 million people. The stats reveals that most of these people are in rich countries. This act claims the life of approximately 8 million people each year. After extensive research, scientists have suggested that tobacco smoking could claim up to one billion people over the 21st century. It is also responsible for some respiratory diseases like lung cancer and could cause an increase blood pressure and heart rate.
